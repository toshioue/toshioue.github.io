Who We Are

Koupwehliki OH Wauneki Atail-sohso – Our native language’s expression of “Pride and Respect in Our Island’s Bounties and Culture” strives to convey the intrinsic values of being an environmental steward and knowing that the well-being of our community is inherently tied to the well-being of our environment and the preservation of our unique cultural heritage. Although our islands’ rich culture and history is not as abundantly documented as compared to those of Western societies, the essence our culture and history continues to thrive through our people and environment who continue to be living embodiments of man’s interdependency with nature. We recognize that the future of island communities is dependent on the harmony of nature and the environment around us.

KOHWA holds with utmost conviction that a world, amidst the reality of a changing climate directly influenced by anthropogenic activities, is not one that we should leave behind for future generations. Either we stand idly by convinced that our efforts on an individual is irrevocably futile, or we struggle, we adapt, and we choose to see that although a single person’s action is but a ripple, together our ripples can become waves that flow to inspire others and crash against our barriers of change. Positive change, lasting change, monumental change – these results accrue to those who are passionate, dedicated, conscientious enough to contend with life’s barrage of disappointment, uncertainty, and adversity. Change that is powerful, meaningful, and embedded takes collective mindful action. KOHWA invites you to take the first steps along with us for the sake of our islands, our people, and our future.
